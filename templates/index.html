ABOUT US

Krop view is a satellite based crop monitoring platform developed by smart-city lab NCAI to help farmers and agriculture workers. The purpose of Smartcity Lab is to enable applications for precision agriculture in order to sustain farming.

We are working to bring innovative technologies for developing countries so that farms can sustain with minimum input and cut back on their resource wastage. One of our product is soil probe which are currently deployed at different farms across Pakistan. The probes delivers a deep understanding of the Agri-soil offering real time and precision monitoring at multiple depths.

For precise application of pesticides, accurate monitoring of pest infesting crops is necessary. In order to make this process efficient we have automated the process using pest detection device. The camera placed in the device captures pictures of yellow card having pest in periodic manner. The pictures are sent on the server where farmers can monitor their pest infested fields.

Satellite imagery for agriculture

Satellite imagery can be a useful tool in agriculture, as it allows farmers to obtain detailed, high-resolution images of their fields from above. These images can be used to identify features of the landscape, including soil types, vegetation, and topography, which are useful for crop management.

Precision agriculture for development

By using satellite imagery and other data sources, a crop monitoring platform can help farmers to better predict crop yields, which can help them to optimize their farming practices and make more informed decisions about how to allocate resources.

NDVI and its usage

Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) is a standardized index used to measure the amount of vegetation present in an area. NDVI is useful as it can be used to assess the health and productivity of crops, forests, and other vegetation.
